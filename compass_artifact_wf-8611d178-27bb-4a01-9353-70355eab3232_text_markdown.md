### Bridgewater Pure Alpha II: Anatomy of a 34% year

Bridgewater's record performance stemmed from **three key positioning shifts**:

**Q1-Q2 Positioning:** The fund capitalized on bond and currency volatility spurred by trade war escalation. When German 10-year bonds experienced their largest weekly yield sell-off since 1990 in March, Pure Alpha was positioned to benefit. Currency exposure captured Fed/ECB/BOJ policy divergence.

**Q3 Pivotal Reallocation:** SEC 13F filings reveal dramatic repositioning:
- Complete exit from **16 China positions** ($1.41B in US-listed Chinese stocks sold)
- Exited gold (GLD holdings liquidated)
- **93% reduction** in emerging market ETFs
- NVIDIA cut 65%, Microsoft 36%, Alphabet 50%+
- Increased **U.S. broad exposure** (IVV), AI infrastructure plays (Lam Research, Adobe, ServiceNow)

**Risk Parity Advantage:** All Weather's 20% return demonstrated that risk parity—spreading risk across uncorrelated assets—provided downside protection during April's volatility while capturing upside in H2.

**Extractable Lessons for $5-10M Manager:**
1. Maintain flexibility to dramatically shift positioning
2. Monitor policy divergence across central banks as primary signal
3. Use volatility spikes as entry points rather than panic moments
4. Build systematic rebalancing rules into risk parity approaches

## Part 2: Strategy-specific analysis

### Global macro dominated through central bank divergence

Global macro emerged as 2025's top-performing strategy, with the PivotalPath Global Macro Index returning **+8.6%** while top discretionary macro funds delivered 25-35%.

**Winning macro themes:**

**1. Tariff-driven positioning:** FX trading reached **$9.5 trillion daily turnover** in April 2025 (+27% from April 2022), with $1.5 trillion attributed to tariff-related trading. Successful funds:
- Shorted U.S. equities ahead of tariff announcements
- Long selected Chinese tech names (contrarian)
- Tactical U.S. fixed income positions
- Long gold (65% return in USD terms per Ray Dalio's analysis)

**2. Currency divergence:** Fed (3.50-3.75%) vs. ECB (2.00%) vs. BOJ (0.75%) created a 150-200 bps differential opportunity. Swiss franc (+14% vs. USD) and Japanese yen appreciation rewarded long positions.

**3. Bond market volatility:** 30-year Treasury yields exceeded 5%—the steepest rise since 1981. The spread between 10-year Treasury yields and swaps widened to **64 basis points—the largest on record**. Yield curve steepeners (2s/10s widening) became one of the most profitable trades.

**4. Commodity positioning:** Gold hit record highs on central bank buying and de-dollarization. Copper returned **+42% on LME**—the strongest since 2009. However, pure commodity funds struggled (average -1.5%), demonstrating that macro context matters more than commodity exposure alone.

### Healthcare L/S: The GLP-1 dispersion trade

Healthcare-focused equity funds led all sectors with PivotalPath's Healthcare Index returning **+35.2%**.

**The defining trade:** Long Eli Lilly / Short Novo Nordisk
- **Eli Lilly:** +40% YTD, became first healthcare company to reach **$1 trillion market cap**
- **Novo Nordisk:** -44% YTD, lost GLP-1 leadership, slower growth
- Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) showed 20.2% average weight loss vs. Wegovy's 13.7%

**Additional healthcare alpha sources:**

**Patent cliff M&A:** $180B in revenue at risk 2027-28 drove Big Pharma urgency. Major deals included J&J-Intra-Cellular ($14.6B), Pfizer-Metsera ($10B), Merck-Verona Pharma ($10B).

**Medicare price negotiations:** Semaglutide prices negotiated down 71% for Medicare beneficiaries (effective 2027), creating short opportunities on margin uncertainty.

**Biotech specialist outperformance:** Perceptive Advisors delivered **+75%**, Caligan Partners **+92%**—demonstrating that deep sector expertise trumps generalist approaches in healthcare.

### Quantitative strategies: Divergent performance

Statistical arbitrage led hedge fund rankings with **7.8% YTD** through April (BNP Paribas data), while systematic stock-trading funds gained nearly **12% in H1** (Goldman Sachs). However, trend-following CTAs struggled:

### Risk management framework

- Liquidity: Position size < 1% of average daily volume

**Portfolio-level controls:**
- Gross exposure: 150-200% maximum

- Single sector: <30% concentration
- Daily VaR (95%): 1.5-2% of NAV
- Maximum drawdown tolerance: 15%

**Trigger actions:**
- 5% drawdown: Review all positions, reduce sizing 25%
- 10% drawdown: Cut gross exposure 50%
- 15% drawdown: Consider returning capital, halt new positions

### Position sizing methodology

**Recommended framework for $5-10M:**
1. **Base size:** 2-3% of NAV per position ($100-300K)
2. **Conviction adjustment:** Scale 0.5x to 2x based on signal strength
3. **Volatility adjustment:** Inverse vol scaling (ATR-based)
4. **Liquidity adjustment:** Reduce for illiquid names
5. **Portfolio:** 15-25 positions for adequate diversification

**Volatility-targeting formula:**
```
Position Size = (Capital × Target Vol) / Asset Volatility
Example: $5M × 10% target vol / 25% asset vol = $200K position
```

The 2025 hedge fund performance data reveals a clear pattern: **macro flexibility and conviction beat broad diversification in volatile environments**. Bridgewater's 34%, EDL's 30%, and Melqart's 45% all came from concentrated positioning in their best ideas rather than hedged, diversified approaches.

**Strategy:** Systematic global macro with concentrated equity overlay. Build signals for central bank policy divergence, currency positioning, and yield curve trades. Complement with 15-20 position equity L/S book focused on healthcare (GLP-1 continuation, patent cliff M&A) and value rotation opportunities.

**Operations:** Lean infrastructure using IBKR, outsourced admin/CCO, and custom Python risk tools. Budget $125-150K Year 1. Target breakeven on management fees at $10M, sustainability at $25M+.

**Fundraising:** Start with personal capital + F&F (founder class at 1.25%/15%). Build 18-month audited track record. Target FoF and seeder conversations at $10M AUM. Leverage prime broker capital intro aggressively.

**Risk management:** Position limits (3-5% max), daily VaR monitoring, drawdown triggers at 5%/10%/15%. Survive first to thrive later.

The industry is entering its strongest inflow period since 2017, with two-thirds of allocators open to emerging managers under $100M. The window is open for differentiated, well-operated funds to capture institutional capital. The 2025 winners demonstrated that edge comes from conviction, flexibility, and disciplined risk management—all achievable at small scale with the right framework.
