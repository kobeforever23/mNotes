BAU Stress Testing Scenario Inventory

Companion Guide & Reference Documentation
Enterprise Risk Management Production Version 1.0

1 Executive Overview

This dashboard is a production-grade BAU (Business-as-Usual) Stress Testing Scenario Inventory of the kind operated by Tier 1 dealer banks and large multi-strategy hedge funds. It centralizes 59 fully-populated scenarios spanning five category types, four severity tiers, and 34 risk factors, providing an integrated view of the firm's stress-testing programme across market risk, credit risk, treasury/ALM, and enterprise risk.

The inventory serves as the single source of truth for scenario definitions, shock calibrations, correlation assumptions, and regulatory mappings. It is designed to support daily risk reporting, quarterly CCAR/DFAST submissions, annual BoE ACS compliance, internal model validation, and ad-hoc scenario requests from the CRO or Board.

Dashboard Capabilities: Interactive filtering by category, severity, horizon, asset class, and status · Full-text search · Sortable columns · Expandable detail panels with 34-factor shock grids · Correlation assumptions · Analytics & coverage views · Correlation regime scatter map

2 Scenario Categories

Every scenario belongs to one of five categories. Each serves a distinct purpose in the risk framework.

CategoryCountPurpose & Description
Historical Replay 25 Replays actual market events with observed shock magnitudes. Anchored to real data; gives traders and risk managers an intuitive reference point. Examples: Black Monday 1987, GFC 2008, COVID-19 Crash, Yen Carry Unwind Aug 2024, Liberation Day Tariff Shock Apr 2025.
Hypothetical 13 Forward-looking scenarios that have not occurred but are plausible. Designed to capture emerging risks the historical record may not cover. Examples: China-Taiwan Kinetic Conflict, AI Bubble Burst, Cyber Attack on Financial Infrastructure, US Sovereign Downgrade to A.
Sensitivity 14 Parametric single-factor or multi-factor shocks used for daily risk decomposition and limit monitoring. Not tied to a narrative. Examples: Parallel Rate Shift +100bp, Equity Shock −25%, VIX Spike to 60, Correlation Spike to 0.95.
Regulatory 4 Mandated by regulators for capital adequacy testing. Prescribed shock parameters and horizons. Examples: Fed CCAR 2025 Severely Adverse, Fed CCAR 2026 Proposed, Fed Exploratory (5 Hedge Fund Failures), BoE ACS 2025 Traded Risk.
Reverse Stress 3 Works backward from a failure threshold (e.g., CET1 breach, liquidity exhaust, P&L stop-loss) to identify what market conditions would cause it. Required under Basel III / ICAAP. Examples: Capital Breach Scenario, Liquidity Exhaust Scenario, P&L Stop-Loss Trigger.

3 Severity Tier Definitions

Severity tiers are calibrated to return-period equivalents. This ensures consistent shock magnitude expectations across the scenario library and aligns with industry standard practices used by regulators, internal model validation, and economic capital frameworks.

SeverityReturn PeriodProbabilityPractical Interpretation
Moderate 1-in-10 year ~10% annual You will likely live through several of these in a career. Think typical corrections: 2015 China deval, Taper Tantrum 2013, Q4 2018 selloff. Used for sensitivity testing and daily limit monitoring. Traders should be able to ride through these within normal risk appetite.
Severe 1-in-25 year ~4% annual CCAR / DFAST "Severely Adverse" calibration level. Once-per-generation event that causes material P&L damage and may trigger risk appetite reviews. Examples: Asian Crisis 1997, European Debt Crisis, SVB Collapse. Capital planning baseline — the firm must demonstrate survival.
Extreme 1-in-100 year ~1% annual GFC/Lehman-class events. Multiple asset classes break simultaneously. Correlation assumptions fail. Market functioning impaired. Examples: Black Monday 1987, GFC 2008, COVID-19 Crash, Dot-Com Crash. Used for economic capital, recovery planning, and board-level stress reporting.
Tail Beyond historical <1% annual Scenarios that exceed anything in the observable historical record. These are not calibrated to data — they are designed to break the model. Examples: US Sovereign Downgrade to A, Eurozone Breakup, China-Taiwan Kinetic Conflict, Cyber Attack on SWIFT/FedWire. Used for reverse stress testing, resolution planning, and identifying single points of failure.
Note on Tail Severity: These scenarios are intentionally beyond historical calibration. Their purpose is not predictive accuracy but rather identification of concentration vulnerabilities and portfolio breaking points. If a Tail scenario produces a modest P&L impact, it signals either strong resilience or under-calibrated shocks — both worth investigating.

4 Risk Factor Shock Grid — Convention Guide

Each scenario defines shocks across 34 risk factors organized into 8 groups. Understanding the sign and unit conventions is critical for correct interpretation.

GroupFactorsUnitSign Convention
Equity Indices S&P 500, NASDAQ, EuroStoxx 50, Nikkei 225, FTSE 100, MSCI EM, CSI 300, Russell 2000 % Negative = index decline. −25% means the index falls 25% from current level.
Equity Volatility VIX Absolute level The number shown is the VIX level (not a change). VIX at 80 = extreme implied vol (cf. GFC peak 80, COVID peak 82).
US Rates UST 2Y, 5Y, 10Y, 30Y, SOFR Basis points (bp) Positive = yields rise (bearish for bonds). +250bp means the yield curve shifts up 250bp. Negative = rally.
Credit Spreads CDX.IG, CDX.HY, CDX.EM Basis points (bp) Positive = spreads widen (credit deteriorates). +580bp on IG = CCAR-level stress. Negative = spread tightening.
FX DXY, EURUSD, USDJPY, GBPUSD, USDCNH % DXY positive = USD strengthens. For pairs: positive EURUSD = EUR appreciates. Positive USDJPY = JPY weakens.
Commodities WTI, Brent, Gold, Silver, Copper, Natural Gas % Negative = price decline. +60% WTI = oil spike (e.g., Hormuz closure scenario).
Crypto / Funding Bitcoin, Repo Rate, TED Spread % (BTC), bp (Repo/TED) Repo: positive = funding stress (repo rate spikes). TED spread: positive = interbank stress.
Structured Products MBS, CMBS, CLO Basis points (bp) Positive = spread widening (mark-to-market losses). +400bp CMBS = severe CRE distress scenario.

5 Correlation Regime Assumptions

Each scenario includes 6 correlation parameters that define the assumed inter-asset co-movement regime. These are critical because standard VaR/CVaR models use historical correlations that break down in stress.

ParameterRangeInterpretation
Stock-Bond (sb) −0.8 to +0.95 Negative = flight-to-quality (bonds hedge equities). Positive = stagflation/rate-shock regime (both sell off). This single parameter defines whether your 60/40 portfolio works.
Equity-Credit (ec) 0.2 to 0.95 How tightly equity drawdowns and credit spread widening co-move. >0.8 = credit can't diversify equity risk (GFC, COVID). Normal environment ~0.3-0.5.
FX-Rates (fr) −0.3 to +0.8 Linkage between FX moves and rate moves. High positive = carry-trade regime (rate hike = currency strengthen). Negative = risk-off (currency weakens despite rates rising on fiscal fears).
Commodity-Equity (ce) −0.5 to +0.8 Positive = growth-linked (demand shock: equities and commodities fall together). Negative = supply shock (oil spikes while equities sell off, stagflation).
Vol-Spot (vs) −0.95 to +0.2 Almost always negative (VIX goes up when stocks go down). When approaching −0.95, implies extreme gamma selling pressure. Values near 0 signal vol detachment (unusual, often pre-crisis).
Contagion Multiplier (cn) 0.2 to 2.0 Scalar amplifying cross-asset contagion. 1.0 = normal transmission. >1.5 = cascade risk (margin calls triggering forced selling across unrelated assets). GFC/COVID peaked near 1.9.

Correlation Regime Map (Scatter Plot)

The dashboard's Correlation Map tab plots every scenario on an S&P 500 vs. UST 10Y grid, creating four quadrants:

Top-Left: Stagflation

Equities fall while rates rise. Stock-bond correlation is positive. The 60/40 portfolio fails. This is the worst-case regime for most institutional portfolios. Examples: 2022 Rate Shock, Global Stagflation, Liberation Day Tariff Shock.

Bottom-Left: Risk-Off

Equities fall and rates fall (flight to quality). Bonds act as a hedge. The standard diversification assumption holds. Examples: Black Monday 1987, GFC 2008, COVID-19 Crash.

Bottom-Right: Goldilocks

Equities rise while rates fall. Central bank easing supports both asset classes. Optimal for leveraged portfolios.

Top-Right: Reflation

Equities rise with rates. Growth-driven tightening cycle. Equities tolerate higher rates because earnings growth is strong.

6 Scenario Horizons & Timing

The horizon defines how quickly the shock materializes, which directly impacts hedging feasibility and liquidity assumptions.

HorizonTimeframeImplication
InstantaneousSame-dayNo hedging possible. Pure mark-to-market. Relevant for flash crashes, VIX spikes, gap risk.
Short-term (1-5d)1 to 5 trading daysLimited hedging window. Liquidity may be impaired. Most relevant for trading book risk.
Medium (1-3m)1 to 3 monthsPortfolio can adjust, but market dislocations persist. Basis risk may dominate. Margin calls compound.
Extended (3-12m)3 to 12 monthsSecular shift. Business model impact. Revenue compression. Credit migration crystallizes.
Full Cycle (9Q)~2.25 yearsCCAR supervisory horizon. Complete economic cycle through peak unemployment and recovery. Captures credit losses that only emerge with lag.

7 Regulatory Framework Mapping

Scenarios are tagged to one or more regulatory frameworks. This allows quick identification of which scenarios satisfy specific compliance requirements.

FrameworkJurisdictionKey Requirements
CCAR / DFAST US (Fed) Annual Comprehensive Capital Analysis and Review. 9-quarter horizon. Severely Adverse scenario prescribed by the Fed (unemployment 10%, equity −33%, CRE −30% in 2025; CRE −40% in proposed 2026). Exploratory scenarios added (e.g., 5 hedge fund failures). Results determine capital distributions.
FRTB Global (Basel) Fundamental Review of the Trading Book. Expected Shortfall replacing VaR. Stressed calibration window. Internal Models Approach (IMA) requires a comprehensive stress testing programme covering the trading book.
Basel III Global Pillar 2 ICAAP stress testing. Reverse stress testing requirement. Liquidity stress (LCR/NSFR under stress). Capital buffers calibrated to stress results.
BoE PRA UK Annual Cyclical Scenario (ACS). Biennial Exploratory Scenario (BES). 2025 ACS focuses on global trade fragmentation and geopolitical escalation with 2-5 day extreme moves.
Internal Firm-level Scenarios developed by the firm's risk management for internal capital adequacy, limit setting, risk appetite monitoring, and ad-hoc analysis. All 59 scenarios carry an Internal tag.
CCAR 2025 vs 2026: The dashboard includes both the current CCAR 2025 parameters (CRE −30%, BBB spread 580bp) and the proposed 2026 parameters (CRE −40%, BBB spread 570bp). The 2026 scenario is tagged Under Review until finalized by the Federal Reserve.

8 Dashboard Navigation Guide

Scenario Table (Main View)

  • Search: Free-text search filters by scenario name, ID, or narrative content.
  • Filter chips: Click category, severity, horizon, asset class, or status chips to narrow results. Multiple selections within a filter group use OR logic; across groups, AND logic.
  • Sort: Click any column header to sort ascending/descending.
  • Expand: Click any row to reveal the full detail panel with the 34-factor shock grid, correlation assumptions, metadata, and historical analogues.
  • KPI bar: Top-line metrics update in real-time based on active filters: total active scenarios, category counts, asset class coverage score, and count under review.

Analytics & Coverage Tab

  • Severity distribution: Bar chart showing count of scenarios per severity tier.
  • Category distribution: Bar chart showing count per category type.
  • Risk factor frequency: Horizontal bar chart ranking which risk factors are most commonly stressed across all 59 scenarios. Useful for identifying factors with thin coverage.
  • Coverage heatmap: Asset class (rows) vs. category (columns). Red cells indicate coverage gaps where no scenarios stress that intersection.

Correlation Map Tab

  • Scatter plot: Each dot = one scenario, plotted on S&P 500 shock (x-axis) vs. UST 10Y move (y-axis). Color = category.
  • Hover: Hover over any dot to see the scenario name and exact shock values.
  • Quadrant labels: Stagflation (top-left), Risk-Off (bottom-left), Goldilocks (bottom-right), Reflation (top-right).

9 Scenario Sub-Category Taxonomy

Each scenario is assigned a sub-category reflecting the primary risk driver. The inventory covers 10 distinct sub-types:

Equity Crash

Primary driver is a sharp equity drawdown. Growth sectors, index-level, or factor-specific (e.g., value rotation).

Rates Shock

Driven by yield curve moves — parallel shifts, steepeners, flatteners, or breakeven repricing.

Credit Blowout

Spread widening across IG, HY, EM, or structured credit. May include fallen angel events.

FX Crisis

Currency devaluations, carry-trade unwinds, EM contagion, or DXY dislocation.

Vol Explosion

VIX/MOVE spikes. Vol surface repricing. Structured product hedging stress. Gamma squeeze dynamics.

Liquidity Freeze

Funding markets seize. Repo, prime brokerage, or settlement failures. Bid-ask spreads blow out.

Geopolitical

Military conflict, trade wars, sanctions, terrorism. Non-economic shock origination with market contagion.

Sovereign

Government debt/rating stress. Fiscal crises. Eurozone redenomination risk.

Contagion

Multi-vector crisis where the defining feature is cascading cross-market transmission.

Stagflation

Simultaneous growth contraction and inflation acceleration. Monetary policy paralysis.

10 Governance, Review & Approval

Each scenario carries metadata reflecting its governance lifecycle:

  • Status: Active (production-ready), Under Review (pending approval — e.g., CCAR 2026 proposed), Retired (archived, no longer run).
  • Approval: Approved by MRC (Market Risk Committee), Approved by CRO, Approved by Board — reflects the seniority of sign-off required based on scenario severity and scope.
  • Last Run: The date the scenario was last executed against the firm's portfolio. Staleness beyond 90 days should trigger a review.
  • Review Frequency: How often the scenario's calibration is reviewed — Quarterly, Semi-Annual, or Annual. Regulatory scenarios align with submission cycles.
  • Run Frequency: How often the scenario is executed against the firm's current positions — Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Quarterly, or Annual.
  • Owner: The risk function responsible for maintaining the scenario and its calibration (e.g., Market Risk - Equities, Enterprise Risk, Treasury/ALM).
Review Cadence: Scenarios should be recalibrated when: (1) significant market events render shocks unrealistic, (2) regulatory parameters change, (3) the firm's portfolio composition shifts materially, or (4) model validation identifies calibration drift. The CRO must approve all Extreme/Tail tier recalibrations.
