The Catalyst

What Happened

Altruist, a tech-forward wealth platform for independent advisors, launched a new AI-powered tax planning feature within its platform Hazel this morning. The tool reads and interprets clients' 1040s, paystubs, account statements, meeting notes, emails, and custodial/CRM data, then applies proprietary tax logic to generate fully personalized tax strategies — all within minutes.

The feature includes interactive scenario modeling for real-time "what-if" tax projections. It's available to any advisory firm regardless of custody arrangements at $60/seat/month standalone, making it an immediate competitive threat to incumbents across the wealth management industry.

"Hazel's tax planning feature flips that dynamic. It expands what a single advisor can handle, raises the bar on outcomes, and makes average advice a lot harder to justify."

— Jason Wenk, Founder & CEO of Altruist (ex-Morgan Stanley)

Market Impact

The Selloff

Wealth management stocks suffered their worst single-day declines since April. LPL Financial fell as much as 11% intraday before closing down 8.31%. The selloff caught Wall Street off guard — most analysts had maintained positive ratings on the affected names heading into the session.

"The sell-off appears tied to broader concerns about AI disrupting the financial advice and wealth-management model."

— Neil Sipes, Bloomberg Intelligence Analyst

Notably, LPL Financial's own head of equity research Thomas Shipp had written just last week that "fewer technical users are now empowered to replace existing workflows" with AI tools — and then his firm's stock got hammered on exactly that thesis today.

Pattern Recognition

The Rolling AI Disruption Trade

Today's wealth management selloff is the latest in a sector-by-sector rotation of the "AI disruption" narrative. Investors have been systematically repricing incumbents as new AI tools emerge targeting specific verticals:

Late January / Early February 2026
Software stocks hit first. Anthropic's Claude tools (including Cowork) triggered massive selloffs. Thomson Reuters plunged 15.83% (worst day on record). LegalZoom sank 19.68%. FactSet fell 10.51%. Software ETFs posted worst day since April.
Week of Feb 3, 2026
Private credit firms hit. AI-driven investment analysis tools sparked fears about automated fund management and credit underwriting. Blue Owl (OWL) fell 9.76%.
Monday, Feb 9, 2026
Insurance brokerages hit. AI disruption fears rotated into insurance distribution and brokerage names.
Tuesday, Feb 10, 2026 — Today
Wealth management hit. Altruist's Hazel AI tax planning tool triggers 7-9% selloff across Schwab, Raymond James, LPL, Stifel. Worst day since April for all four.

Key Pattern: Sharp Selloff → Partial Bounce

In prior rotations, affected stocks have typically rebounded 1-2% the following day as dip buyers step in. Thomson Reuters and LegalZoom both bounced ~1% the day after their respective drops. Watch for a similar dynamic in SCHW, LPLA, RJF, and SF on Wednesday.

Risk Desk Implications

CIB & Trading Desk Considerations

Contagion Risk — Who's Next?

The rotation pattern suggests asset management and retail banking could be next if new AI tools target those verticals. Any CIB desk with concentrated exposure to financial advisory names should be monitoring for additional AI product launches that could trigger the next leg of the rotation.

Volatility & VaR Impact

Single-day moves of 7-11% in large-cap financial names (Schwab: ~$100B market cap) create meaningful P&L volatility. Desks holding wealth management names or correlated financials may see elevated VaR contributions. The intraday swing in LPLA (−11% midday → −8.3% close) also signals high intraday volatility that may not be fully captured in end-of-day risk metrics.

Structural vs. Sentiment

The fundamental question is whether Hazel-type tools truly threaten wealth management revenues or whether this is sentiment-driven repricing. Tax planning is one component of wealth advisory — it doesn't replace portfolio management, estate planning, behavioral coaching, or relationship management. But it does commoditize a high-margin service line, and the market is pricing in the risk that more capabilities follow.

Big Tech Tax Angle

Separately, Amazon, Meta, and Alphabet all reported significant drops in federal tax bills today due to AI datacenter investments combined with OBBBA depreciation and R&D provisions. This highlights how the AI buildout is creating both disruption (wealth mgmt selloff) and tax efficiency (Big Tech tax reduction) simultaneously.
