## Risk analysis specific to AI businesses

Building an AI business in 2025 requires clear-eyed assessment of risks that didn't exist for traditional SaaS.

**API dependency is real but manageable**: OpenAI's GPT-4 Turbo dropped from $25/$100 to $10/$30 per million tokens in 2024, then to $2.50/$10 for GPT-4o. Older models consistently get cheaper. But frontier reasoning models (o1) cost $15/$60—so if competitors use better models, your margins compress. **Mitigation**: Build abstraction layers enabling instant switching between OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and open-source models. Llama 4 and Mistral 3 now achieve "good enough" quality for 80%+ of business applications, providing insurance against API pricing changes.

**Model obsolescence killed products in 12 months**: Chegg's stock collapsed 95% when students switched to ChatGPT. OpenAI's built-in retrieval made many RAG startups obsolete overnight. Document chat tools that raised millions in 2023 were commoditized by 2024. **Mitigation**: Don't build thin wrappers. Own proprietary data, embed deeply in workflows, and choose markets requiring domain expertise (like regulated finance) where generic AI can't compete.

**Open-source is both threat and opportunity**: DeepSeek R1 and V3 match GPT-4 at a fraction of the cost with open weights. Qwen 3 now has more downloads than Llama. Basic text generation, translation, and summarization are commoditizing. **What remains valuable**: domain-specific fine-tuning with proprietary data, workflow orchestration, industry compliance knowledge, and human-in-the-loop systems for high-stakes decisions.

**Regulatory risk is accelerating**: The EU AI Act begins full enforcement in August 2026 with penalties up to €35M or 7% of global revenue. Colorado's AI Act (February 2026) requires "reasonable care" to avoid algorithmic discrimination. For financial services, you'll also face scrutiny from SEC, OCC, and CFPB on AI decision-making. **Mitigation**: Document AI decision processes from day one, maintain audit trails, and position compliance as a feature (your finance background is an asset here).

**Big tech is not entering your niche**: Microsoft, Google, and OpenAI are focused on horizontal platforms (Copilot, Gemini, ChatGPT). They're not building stress testing tools for community banks. Vertical AI for regulated industries remains safe from big tech entry because the market is too small and requires too much domain expertise for their attention.

## Legal structure and tax optimization

**Start with Delaware LLC, convert to C-Corp later**: This maximizes QSBS (Qualified Small Business Stock) potential. If you form an LLC, build value to $10-40M, then convert to C-Corp, your cost basis becomes the FMV at conversion. The QSBS exclusion is the greater of $10M or **10x your basis**—so a $40M basis at conversion creates potential $400M tax-free exclusion on future sale.

**QSBS is critical for AI founders**: The tax benefit can be worth millions. Requirements: domestic C-Corporation, gross assets under $50M at stock issuance, hold for 5+ years. As of July 2025, the exclusion is tiered: 50% at 3 years, 75% at 4 years, 100% at 5 years.

**R&D tax credit**: AI model development, software engineering, and testing qualify. The credit is 20% of qualified expenses and can offset payroll taxes for startups under $250K gross receipts. Document time spent on development meticulously.

**AI-specific insurance is essential**: Standard tech E&O policies often **exclude algorithmic decisions**. Get explicit AI endorsements. Budget $10K-25K annually for proper coverage (Tech E&O, Cyber Liability, D&O) once generating revenue.

**Startup cost estimate**:
- Entity formation: $500-1,500
- Operating costs (months 1-6): $1,500-3,000/month
- MVP development: $15,000-40,000
- Marketing/launch: $2,000-5,000
- Legal/accounting: $3,000-5,000
- **Total to launch**: $35,000-65,000
